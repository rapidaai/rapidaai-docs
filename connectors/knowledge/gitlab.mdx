The GitLab connector connects Rapida to your GitLab repositories, enabling your assistant to reference source code, documentation, and wikis when answering developer questions.

## Prerequisites

- A GitLab.com account with access to the repositories you want to index
- The Rapida administrator must have configured `gitlab_client_id` and `gitlab_client_secret` in the OAuth settings

## Connecting GitLab

Follow these steps to connect GitLab to Rapida:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Connectors page">
    In the Rapida dashboard, navigate to **Integrations → Connectors** and select **GitLab** under Knowledge Connectors.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Authorize Rapida">
    Click **Connect**. You will be redirected to GitLab's authorization screen. Sign in and approve the access request.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select repositories">
    After authorizing, select the repositories you want to include in your knowledge base.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Attach to a knowledge base">
    Navigate to your knowledge base, click **Add Source**, and select your GitLab connection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How It Works

Rapida uses the GitLab OAuth 2.0 authorization code flow to obtain a token with repository access. The token is stored securely in your Rapida Vault. Selected repository content is then indexed for retrieval.

## Permissions

| Scope | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| `repo` | Read access to repositories |

## Limitations

- Only GitLab.com (cloud) is supported. Self-hosted GitLab instances are not currently supported.
- Repository content is indexed at the time of connection. New commits require a manual re-sync.
